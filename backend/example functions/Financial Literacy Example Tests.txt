From the FINRA Financial Test


Suppose you have X in Savings Account earning Y % interest in a year. After Z years, how much would you have.

1.More than X+Y*X
2. Exactly X+Y*X
3. Less than X+Y*X
4. Don't Know 

More than X+Y*X is always the answer


 Imagine that the interest rate on a savings account is X percent a year and inflation is Y percent a year. After Z year(s), would the money in the account buy more than it does today, exactly the same or less than today?
 
 A. More
 B. Same
 C. Less
 D. Don't Know
 
 If X = Y Same
 If X > Y More
 If X < Y Less
